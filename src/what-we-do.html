What We Do

Breast Cancer Awareness Campaigns

We organize talks, workshops, and community forums to educate women and men about breast cancer â€” its risks, myths, and the importance of early detection.

Breast Self-Examination (BSE) Training

We teach women and girls how to check their own breasts through live demos and printed guides in local languages.

Free Screening Days

We partner with hospitals and clinics to provide free breast screening services, especially during Pink October and other special health days.

Support Groups for Patients & Survivors

We run safe spaces where women affected by breast cancer can share their stories, encourage each other, and find emotional strength.

Hospital Visits

Our volunteers regularly visit breast cancer patients in hospital wards to offer hope, prayers, and small care packages.

Pink October Campaigns

Every October, we lead national marches, radio shows, social media campaigns, and school events to raise awareness.

Educational Outreach in Schools & Communities

We talk to students, teachers, and community leaders about breast cancer, fighting stigma and misinformation.

Advocacy and Policy Engagement

We work to bring the voices of survivors and patients to policymakers, calling for better screening, treatment, and patient support services in The Gambia.

Collaboration & Partnerships

We collaborate with hospitals, NGOs, ministries, and international partners to strengthen the fight against breast cancer.

Memorial and Tribute Events

We honor the lives of those lost to breast cancer and celebrate survivors to keep hope alive in the community.
